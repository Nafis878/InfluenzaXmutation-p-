=== Temporal Split Rationale (v2) ===

Problem: Original Scenario A test window (2019-2021) yielded only 30 mutations from
the 2000-sample balanced mutation dataset (25 from 2019, 5 from 2020, 0 from 2021).
All 30 belong to a single class (H3N2 drift-positive), making AUC undefined.

Root cause: The 2000-sample balanced dataset prioritized class balance over temporal
diversity. Year 2019 was the tail of the dataset with almost no H1N1 sequences,
and 2021+ was beyond the dataset's temporal coverage (max year = 2020).

Chosen splits (v2):
  Scenario A (Conservative):
    train: first_year < 2006
    test:  first_year in [2006, 2009]  (4-year window)
    n_test mutations in window: 160 + 68 + 91 + 206 = 525
    Rationale: Y_A=2006 is the first year where a 4-year window contains >= 100 mutations.
    This window also spans the H5N1 threat era and early H1N1 surveillance expansion,
    representing a meaningful temporal generalization challenge.

  Scenario B (Standard):
    train: first_year < 2010
    test:  first_year >= 2010
    n_test mutations: ~679
    Rationale: Y_B=2010 is the first post-pandemic year, providing a natural
    boundary for temporal holdout. The 2009 pandemic H1N1 creates a structural
    break in viral evolution patterns.

  Year-by-year AUC curve:
    Test years evaluated: first_year in {2006, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012,
                                         2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018}
    All have >= 30 mutations per year in the balanced dataset.
    Years < 30 mutations: SKIPPED (marked as insufficient_data in plot).
